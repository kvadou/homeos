# GatherRoot Phase 5 — Monetization Validation

**Date:** July 15, 2026  
**Status:** Research mode; charging disabled  
**Decision owner:** Founder

## Objective

Learn whether activated households understand and will pay for GatherRoot's
compounding value without weakening trust, activation, or ownership of their
home record. Phase 5 validates packaging and willingness to pay before wiring a
payment processor or App Store products.

## Recommended package hypothesis

### Free — the complete home record

- One home and its items, documents, projects, care history, and timeline
- View, edit, correct, delete, and export all user-created records
- Basic care schedule and grounded answers from saved records
- Safety information and account/privacy controls

These capabilities are never held hostage by a subscription.

### GatherRoot Plus — ongoing household intelligence

- Proactive Home Briefings, warranty and lifecycle intelligence
- Deeper cited Ask reasoning and advanced home reports
- Household collaboration and handoff workflows
- Connected-source imports and future integrations
- Concierge provider coordination when available in the pilot market
- Additional properties as a separately tested value axis

**Price hypothesis:** $99/year primary or $12/month. This is research input,
not a published or charged price. Provider-side pricing remains a separate
post-pilot question.

## Upgrade rules

1. Never interrupt onboarding, safety guidance, data editing, or export.
2. Show an upgrade decision only after first value: three real records plus an
   intelligence action, or when the user explicitly opens Membership.
3. Explain the paid outcome and the free alternative on the same screen.
4. A dismissal lasts at least 30 days; never manufacture urgency.
5. During research mode, the strongest action records intent. It does not ask
   for payment details or imply a trial has started.

## Research protocol

Capture the presented price, billing period, activation state, value theme, and
one of: not now, maybe, likely, or request early access. Ask an optional
open-text reason only after the choice. Follow with 15–20 interviews using Van
Westendorp's four price questions and a short MaxDiff exercise across proactive
intelligence, household collaboration, integrations, reports, multiple homes,
and provider coordination.

## Charging gate

Charging remains disabled until all are true:

- at least 20 activated-household responses and 10 interviews;
- users can distinguish Free from Plus without believing their data is at risk;
- at least 30% of activated respondents choose likely or early access at the
  tested price hypothesis;
- activation and retained use show no material decline after exposure;
- Stripe and App Store product identifiers, restore purchases, cancellation,
  entitlement reconciliation, and legal copy are verified;
- founder explicitly approves the package and price.

## Phase exit gate

Phase 5 exits when evidence supports a price/package decision and the paid
conversion path preserves activation and trust. A weak result means revising
the package or price—not adding pressure to the interface.
